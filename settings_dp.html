Settings over content was once a space where accessibility & usability issues were deeply rooted in unclear hierarchy, ambiguous naming, and lack of user feedback. My goal wasn’t just to improve the UX, but to reimagine settings that scaled across devices and made the UX easier for everyone.

My process began with comprehensive research across multiple inputs — usage analytics, competitive audits, internal audits, and direct user studies. This helped reveal critical pain points in navigation, terminology, and feedback.

From there, I mapped those insights into a framework to redefine the menu architecture, standardize nomenclature, and prioritize features based on usage patterns and cognitive load.

We began the design phase with clear, measurable success criteria to guide decisions and focus testing.

I supported the research by prototyping and iterative design to test layout, navigation, and interaction patterns. Each round was shaped by direct user feedback, helping us refine hierarchy, improve clarity from a distance, and streamline key flows.
